My Personal Statement

This statement outlines the way the applicant believes that he meets the essential and desirable criteria.

Education:

The applicants obtained his Bachelor in Mechanical Engineering from Hainan University, China in 2012, and then MSc degree in Mechatronics from the University of Bath in 2014. After graduated from the University of Bath, the applicant worked in the industry for a short period of time. Determined to experience academic research and pursue higher education, the applicant joined Cardiff University as a Ph.D. student in the High-value manufacturing research group at the School of Engineering in September 2015. His Ph.D. focuses on the research area of manufacturing informatics. his major is slightly changed from mechanical engineering, Mechatronics to manufacturing informatics, which provides him a comprehensive background knowledge of both hardware and software development. Benefiting from his educational background, he is proficient in not only mechanical, electrical, embedded and integrated system design and development but also advanced data analytics in high-value manufacturing. The applicant is expected to finish his Ph.D. in September 2019.

Research Experiences

• Additive manufacturing and advanced data analytics

The applicant formally started his Ph.D. research from 2015 in Mechanical Manufacturing at Cardiff University. His Ph.D. project aims to model, predict, and optimise the energy consumption for AM systems based on the Internet of things (IoT) technologies and advanced data analytics. The main objective is to sense, collect and integrate multi-source data from process, design, environment, and material to build the energy consumption prediction modelling, and then reduce the energy consumption by optimising the design relevant features. He designed and developed an IoT-based process monitoring platform the working environment which integrated with the data collected from the target AM system. The IoT technology is applied in this project extensively, including extra data sensing and collection, wireless communication, and edge data pre-processing. In this project, two energy consumption modelling approaches were proposed based on multi-source data and design-relevant data. These two modelling approaches incorporated clustering techniques and deep learning to integrate the multi-source data and design-relevant data, and then to build the energy consumption prediction models for AM systems. Moreover, an energy consumption optimisation approach was also proposed focusing on the AM part design and process planning, which the particle swarm optimisation was improved by deep learning technology. A set of optimisations suggesting is generated for AM part designers, process operators, and manufactures.

• Internet of things

Another research interest is the Internet of things (IoT). The applicant keens in learning and discovering the knowledge of IoT. The applicant won an IoT competition award, which provided full funding for achieving one of his research proposals. This competition was hosted and organised by Jisc. The project aimed to build the intelligent campus, and improve student satisfaction, recognition and living experience of university, this project built a smart IoT system to deliver the accessible university facilities, such as IoT-enable chairs and desks in public forum and IoT-enable discuss zone in the library, to students. Each IoT device of the system collected the status data of the targeted facility in real-time. The collected raw data was preprocessed at local devices, and the pre-processed data was then uploaded to the target cloud server via the LoRa gateway. The availabilities of university facilities and relevant knowledge was further analysed and discovered on the cloud, which can provide university students the utilisation and management of the accessible resources.

• Processing monitoring, controlling and optimisation

He started his research from the final project of his MSc degree at the University of Bath, which focused on robotic system process monitoring, controlling and optimisation. The project is named ‘Feed-Forward Controller Design for Vibration Suppression in Motion Control Robotic Systems’. This project proved and improved the theories of input shapers which can reduce the vibration of the object carried a robot. The theories were simulated by MATLAB software, and validation experimental was designed and tested on a KUKA industrial robot. The results of the simulation and experiment can justify the function of input shaper in vibration suppression of the robotic systems. During simulation and experimental test, some problems appeared, and appropriate solutions to address these problems were given. This project compares and analyses the results of simulation and experiments objectively. He has worked in two industrial companies for monitoring and controlling system design and development. He was involved in a robot design and development for design the structure of gripper and the monitoring system on the gripper. Another recent project he has joined is to develop a new product for an industrial company, which is an intelligent monitoring and controlling system for manufacturing process. This IoT based data sensing, collection and process platform is self-configurating. When sensors are connected to the platform, the platform can find the IP address automatically and start to collect data from sensor.

• Outcome delivery

Until now, the applicant has published three journal paper, four conference paper and six patents. The quality and the impact of the research outputs produced in his early-stage career is suggested by the 299 citations that they are received from the international research community to date. From 2016 until now, he has attended four international academic conferences. On every conference, he presented his research either by the oral presentation and the poster. Additionally, the applicant has been the reviewer for many academic journals, such as Journal of Mechanical Design (ASME), Journal of Computing and Information Science in Engineering (ASME), and Robotics and Computer-Integrated Manufacturing and conferences, such as ASME 2019 International Design Engineering Technical Conferences (IDETC 2019) and IEEE 2019 International Conference on Automation Science, publication job. The applicant has a strong ability and significant experience in the academic report, and paper writing. Furthermore, he has proved his skills in delivering the research outcomes by different methods, such as verbal presentation, poster, and industrial seminar speaking.

According to the projects he has joined, that shown above, the ability of time management, conceptualise and understand the commercial imperative for the project, problem-solving, and project management has been proved by the applicant.

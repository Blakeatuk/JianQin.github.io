Research Experiences

2015 ~ Present Advanced Data Analytics for AM Energy Modelling, Prediction, and Management under Industry 4.0
School of Engineering, Cardiff University, United Kingdom Postgraduate Research

  • Discovery the knowledge of AM processes and reduce energy consumption.
  • Sense, collect and integrate multi-source data from process, design, environment, and material to build the energy consumption prediction modelling.
  • Design and develop an IoT platform , including extra data sensing and collection, wireless communication (Ad-hoc), and edge data pre-processing.
  • Energy consumption optimisation approach based on the AM part design and process planning.
  • Optimisation suggestions for AM part designers, process operators, and manufactures.
  • Algorithms and hardware are coded by Python.
  • Three conference papers and two journal papers have been published.

2019 ~ present IoT-enabled Facility Monitoring System for Smart University Living Experience
School of Engineering, Cardiff University, United Kingdom Jisc Funded Project

  • Awarded the funding from the IoT competition organised by Jisc and Digital Catapult.
  • A smart IoT system to deliver the accessible university facilities , such as IoT-enable chairs and desks.
  • Collect the status data of the target facility in real-time and be pre-processed at local devices.
  • Data is uploaded to the target cloud server via the LoRa gateway.
  • Availabilities of university facilities and relevant knowledge will be further analysed and discovered.

2014 ~ 2015 High-load Industrial Four-axis Handling Robot
Beijing Research Institute of Automation for Machinery Industry R&D

  • Chinese government-funded project
  • Unique high-load industrial handling robot.
  • Design the gripper f the four-axis robot including the mechanical structure and monitoring system.
  • Monitoring system included a load cell , vibration sensors , speed sensor , accelerometer , and infrared sensors.
  • Several CAD and CAE software were used, such as Auto CAD , SolidWorks , and ANSYS

2014 ~ 2014 Feed-Forward Controller Design for Vibration Suppression in Motion Control Robotic Systems
Department of Mechanical Engineering, Bath University MSc final project

  • Prove and improve the theories of input shapers which can reduce the vibration of the object carried by the industrial robot.
  • Simulated by Simulink in MATLAB , and validation experimental was designed and tested on a KUKA industrial robot.
  • Results of the simulation and experiment can justify the function of input shaper in vibration suppression of the robotic systems.
